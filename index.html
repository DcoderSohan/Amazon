Welcome to Amazon Jungle !!

The Amazon Rainforest, the world's largest tropical rainforest, spans 5.5 million square kilometers across South America. Known for its unparalleled biodiversity,
it houses millions of unique species and the Amazon River.
Dubbed the "lungs of the Earth," it plays a vital role in climate regulation by absorbing carbon dioxide and producing oxygen.
The forest is also home to many indigenous communities who have coexisted with its resources for millennia.
